Visualizing Football Stadiums in the U.K

Insights from stadium data

England and Wales

Spotlight on: Clustering

Using Leaflet's Circle and Polygon features, we can put a focus on the distribution of stadiums in just England and Wales. As one would probably expect, we can see the clustering around the most populated metropolitan areas of London, Manchester and Sheffield (red circles). We can also see a 'coastal belt' of stadiums on the southern coast (blue triangle) - signalling the entertainment value afforded to coastal cities and towns like Brighton and Bournemouth - enhancing the brand value of football clubs that are based there.



Scotland and the 'Northern Lights'

Spotlight on: Night Lights

Here we see focus on Scotland and the norther side of the U.K with a background of 'Earth at Night' imagery from NASA. This juxtaposition confirms our hypothesis from above - of stadiums clustering around brightly lit urban areas. What is intriguing about this visualization is that upon checking the individual stadium capacities of the 'outlier' stadiums on the fringes nearer to the coasts, the stadium capacities (which are in the 1000 - 7000 range) can be said to be a proxy for the overall population of the regions they are in.



Capacity Comparison

Spotlight on: Seating Capacity

Looking at the above comparison of the lowest capacity stadiums (0 - 10,188) and compare it to the largest stadiums (60,073 - 90,000), we see the stark difference in representation by league. At the lower end, the majority of stadiums are occupied by teams classified with an 'Other' league - typically in the Scottish lower leagues amongst which there is tremendous churn - explaining their relegation to the smaller, often publicly funded stadiums. At the upper end of the capacity spectrum, the data is more common-sense - with the large national team stadiums of England and Wales - Wembley and Millenium stadium - making up a large part of the consort along with the biggest Premier League and Scottish Premiership stadiums.



The longest Journey

Spotlight on: Distance between stadiums

After exploring the distances between various stadiums in the respective leagues, I decided to draw a route using Leaflet's Polylines functionality of the longest journey that fans and players have to make in the Premier League - a 359 mile journey between Bournemouth and Newcastle. The visualization shows the cross country trip that essentially covers the breadth of England in one fell swoop. Although the trip only takes about one and a half hours by flight, one can imagine that for die-hard local fans who can't afford flights, the road trip would have to be a multi-day affair. This visualization also makes us appreciate the relative density of football stadiums in the U.K compared to those in other countries - allowing us to visit and enjoy ourselves in sporting venues all around us.


Goal of this Map

The purpose of this map is to give the viewer a high level visualization of football stadiums across the U.K. The aim is to provide a deeper perspective into the breadth of sporting facilities available in the country - allowing the user to filter by team and capacity.
